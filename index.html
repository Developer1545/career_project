A Typical Day:

One daily task is finding and fixing bugs found in the software the programmer is working on. Bugs can be small a easy to fix or they can be hard to find and harder to fix.

Programmers also may collaborate with others on certain projects. If they are a freelance programmer, they may be contacting and working with clients.

And finally, the most important task, writing more code! A programmer may spend lots of time implementing new features or refractoring code to make it better.

3 Concerns

Low Movement and Activity

Most of a programmer's day is spent sitting at a desk and looking at a screen. This can be bad for physical health because the programmer is sitting all day and not moving very oftern. This is changing though because people have realized the health risks. Secondly looking at a screen all day can be bad for your eyes.

Long Hours

Sometimes to meet a deadline a programmer may have to put in more time then they wanted to put in. This can furthermore lead to other things that can affect the programmer's health like lack of sleep. Lack of sleep and longer hours can lead to stress which can hurt your health even more.

Things change really fast

New technolgies are being made everyday. This means that programmers are constantly learning, which as seen in the benefits section can be great, but learning so many new things could get overwelming for some people. Luckily, new technologies sometimes outdate older ones [EDIT].
