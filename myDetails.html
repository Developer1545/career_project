My School Choices

WCSU

Cost: $28,346

Grades and Testing:

At WCSU a GPA of 3.0 is required to get in. SAT test scores are optional. Students who submit without SAT scores must comply with slightly different standards such as having a B average in all classes. You also need to submit other things like letters of recommendation and your first quarter senior year grades. As with RIT, WCSU also looks at your transcript and allows credit for many AP classes taken in High School.

RIT

Cost: $61,474

Grades and Testing:

A GPA of 3.7 or better is High School is required. RIT also would like you to be above average in many of your classes which means that you will need mostly As and Bs. If you show you took AP classes, it shows you challeged yourself more which means RIT may accept a lower GPA. You also have to submit either ACT or SAT scores. Although with the SATs there is really no lowest score, the majority who apply score at least a 1300 on the SAT. A degree could take up to 4 or 5 years.

Equiptment

For both programs a computer is required for your degree.

Aditional Certification

After college, your employer may want you to get certified for specific things. It depends on the job you are doing. For example, an AWS Developer-Associate Certification means that the programmer will have further knowledge working on Amazon's AWS system. Below are common certifications and their cost.

  • AWS Certified Developer-Associate

    • $150 - $300

    As mentioned before, this is a certificate for Amazon's AWS systems. Many developers say they are worth having. Anyone who has had experience working with or is going to be working with Amazon's AWS services will find this certificate useful.

  • Google Cloud Architect Certifications

    • $125 - $200

    Someone working with Google's cloud databases may get this certification. Getting this certification will mean that the developer or programmer will be able to use and understand Google's Cloud services better. Google.com says that 87% of people who got this certification felt better and more confident in the utilization of cloud skills.
